- INTRODUCTION .

Are you interested in understanding the factors that leads to student dropout. Our website is dedicated to providing comprehensive insight and resources for analyzing and addressing this critical issue. Whether you’re an educator, researcher, policymaker, simply curious about dropout rates this is the right place

Student dropout rates in India have been a persistent concern, and their impact on the economy is substantial. High dropout rates lead to a shortage of skilled workers, hindering economic growth. This issue exacerbates unemployment, reduces productivity, and limits India's potential to compete in the global market. To address this, investments in education and policies promoting retention are crucial for India's long-term economic development

Govt. Scheme
  • . Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA):

    : SSA is a government program aimed at providing free and compulsory education to all children up to the age of 14. It focuses on improving access to quality education, reducing dropout rates, and enhancing learning outcomes.

  • Mid-Day Meal Scheme:

    This initiative provides free meals to school children, encouraging enrollment and regular attendance. Proper nutrition can contribute to better concentration and retention, reducing the likelihood of dropout

  • Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA):

    RMSA focuses on improving the quality of secondary education and increasing enrollment by providing financial assistance for infrastructure development and teacher training.

  • Scholarship Programs:

    Various scholarships are available to incentivize students to stay in school and pursue higher education. Examples include the National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship and the Post-Matric Scholarship Scheme

  • Jan Shikshan Sansthan (JSS):

    JSS promotes vocational education and skill development, helping students acquire employable skills, which can deter dropout due to the perception of limited future prospects.

  • Digital Education Initiatives:

    The government has launched digital learning platforms like SWAYAM and SWAYAM PRABHA to make education more accessible, especially in remote areas

  • Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao:

    This campaign emphasizes the importance of educating girls and offers incentives to promote their enrollment and retention in schools.

  • Community Engagement:

    : Local communities and Parent-Teacher Associations (PTAs) are encouraged to actively participate in school management and reduce dropout rates through community mobilization.

  • Inclusive Education:

    Programs like the Inclusive Education for Disabled at Secondary Stage (IEDSS) aim to ensure that students with disabilities receive proper education and support, reducing dropout rates among this vulnerable group

Reasons-

Some reasons for students dropout:

  • 1. Academic challenges: Struggles with coursework and low grades can lead to frustration and dropout.
  • 2. Poverty: The cost of education, including tuition, books, and living expenses, can be a barrier for some students
  • 3. Family responsibilities: Students may have to care for family members or have other family obligations that make attending school difficult.
    (Excluding Multi Tasking Staff /Class IV/Group D employees)
  • 4. Migrant labourers children are most likely to drop school since it is getting hard for them to catch study while migrantion every year or even every month.
  • 5. Health issues: Physical or mental health problems can interfere with a student's ability to attend school regularly
  • 6. Lack of interest: Students who feel disengaged or uninterested in their studies may be more likely to drop out.
  • 7. Substance abuse: Substance abuse issues can lead to academic problems and dropout
  • 8. Work commitments: Some students may need to work to support themselves or their families, making it difficult to attend school
  • 9. School environment: Negative school environments, including bullying or safety concerns, can drive students away.
  • 10. Lack of future prospects: Some students may not see the value of education in relation to their future goals, leading them to drop out
  • 11.Drug use: Drugs taking students dropping out school because drugs can interfere there ability to learn and attend school.
